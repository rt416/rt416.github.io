Biography

I’m a Microsoft Research PhD Scholar in the Department of Computer Science, UCL. I’m supervised by Daniel C. Alexander of the Centre for Medical Image Computing and Antonio Criminisi of Microsoft Research Cambridge.

I am interested in developing high-performance algorithms which are more interpretable and safer to use in medical applications. I also hold passion for designing methods that can cheaply and reliably enhance the quality of medical image data. Our paper on Bayesian super-resolution proposes a method based on probabilistic CNN for enhancing dMR images while providing a reliability metric.

I have also had the fortune to work as a technical consultant since January 2016 for a London based start-up, ThinkSono, which aims to develop a diagnostic software for detection of Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT).
